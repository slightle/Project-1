This house rested in the heart of Sante Fe, echoing its culture with adobe walls and blue accents. Many residents add in touches to personalize the outside.
Adobe houses are made of dried mud brick, which is able to stand earthquakes, intense wind and heat. Because the walls absorb heat, it keeps the inside cool.
Adobe buildings are some of the longest standest structures in civilization, as well as being inexpensive to build, making them a popular choice.
