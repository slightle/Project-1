When walking through the streets of downtown Sante Fe, this type of market is a common site. Many use their time and energy to create art to sell. Many also depend on the money made for their livelihood, as the markets are a large part of daily life. It is also an ode to their heritages, as more than 20% of Native Americans in the United States live in the American Southwest. Traditions are carried through the generations to keep the history alive to be celebrated for years to come. Source: https://www.santafe.org/things-to-do/history-and-culture/native-american-culture/.
Among the items sold, jewelry is most popular, especially because of Sante Fe's rich abundance of turquoise. It has been mined for centuries, began by the Pueblo people and still highly valued today. Source: https://www.santafeproperties.com/blog/turquoise-a-symbol-of-new-mexico.html.
